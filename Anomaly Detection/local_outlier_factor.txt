LOCAL OUTLIER FACTOR (LOF) – ANOMALY DETECTION

------------------------------------------------------------

INTRODUCTION

Local Outlier Factor (LOF) is an unsupervised anomaly detection algorithm.

It detects points that have significantly lower density compared to their neighbors.

Used to identify:
1) Local Outliers
2) Global Outliers

------------------------------------------------------------

OUTLIER TYPES

1) Global Outlier
   - A data point that is far away from all other points.
   - Clearly different from the entire dataset.

2) Local Outlier
   - A data point that is far from its nearby neighbors
   - But may still lie within the overall dataset range.

LOF mainly detects LOCAL OUTLIERS.

------------------------------------------------------------

CORE IDEA

LOF compares the local density of a point with the density of its neighbors.

If the density of a point is much lower than its neighbors,
then the point is considered an OUTLIER.

------------------------------------------------------------

K-NEAREST NEIGHBORS (KNN)

LOF uses K nearest neighbors.

K = number of nearby points used for comparison.

Example:
K = 5

This means each point is compared with its 5 nearest neighbors.

------------------------------------------------------------

STEPS IN LOF ALGORITHM

Step 1:
Choose K (number of nearest neighbors).

Step 2:
Compute K-distance for each point.

Step 3:
Find K-nearest neighbors of each point.

Step 4:
Compute Reachability Distance.

Step 5:
Compute Local Reachability Density (LRD).

Step 6:
Compute LOF score.

------------------------------------------------------------

REACHABILITY DISTANCE

Reachability Distance =

max (K-distance of neighbor, actual distance)

This prevents extremely small distance values.

------------------------------------------------------------

LOCAL REACHABILITY DENSITY (LRD)

LRD measures how dense the neighborhood is.

LRD = 1 / (average reachability distance)

High LRD → high density  
Low LRD → low density

------------------------------------------------------------

LOF SCORE

LOF compares density of point with density of neighbors.

LOF(p) = Average (LRD of neighbors / LRD of point)

Interpretation:

LOF ≈ 1
Point is normal.

LOF > 1
Point is an outlier.

LOF >> 1
Strong outlier.

------------------------------------------------------------

VISUAL INTERPRETATION

Dense cluster
→ normal points

Sparse region
→ potential outliers

Points with much lower density than neighbors
→ Local Outliers

Points far away from entire dataset
→ Global Outliers

------------------------------------------------------------

ADVANTAGES

- Detects local anomalies effectively
- Works well with complex datasets
- No need for labeled data

------------------------------------------------------------

LIMITATIONS

- Sensitive to choice of K
- Computationally expensive for large datasets
- Performance decreases in very high dimensions

------------------------------------------------------------

APPLICATIONS

- Fraud detection
- Network intrusion detection
- Fault detection in machines
- Medical anomaly detection
- Credit card fraud detection

------------------------------------------------------------

SUMMARY

LOF is a density-based anomaly detection method.

It:
- Uses K-nearest neighbors
- Computes local density
- Compares density with neighbors
- Assigns LOF score

Higher LOF score → Higher chance of anomaly.
